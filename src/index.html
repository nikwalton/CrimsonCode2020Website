FAQS

What Is CrimsonCode?

CrimsonCode is a 24 hour collegiate Hackathon held at Washington State University in Pullman, WA.

Who Can Attend?

Students of all majors and coding experience are encouraged to attend. Mentors will be availble onsite to help you learn new technologies and give guidance on your project.

What Should I Bring?

A laptop (or desktop), chargers (laptops, phones, tablets, etc), power strip, student ID and an excitment for learning new things. Also bringing a toothbrush, change of clothes, and a blanket or sleeping bag is highly recommended.

How Big Are Teams?

At CrimsonCode we only allow teams of 4 in order to have teams composed of multiple academic backgrounds. We encourage students to work with some people they don't know.

What If I Don't Have A Team?

In the weeks leading up to CrimsonCode, We will be hosting events to help people find teams. Please check our facebook for information on when these team-building events occur.

What If I Don't Have Any Ideas But Still Want to Participate?

Before the Hackathon we will have events that will give example ideas and have brainstorming sessions for potential participants to come up with ideas. Please check our facebook for information on when these brainstorming sessions will be happening.

Can I Work On My Project Beforehand?

Nope. We encourage every team to start a brand new project at CrimsonCode. An exception to this is you are allowed to develop libraries or use libraries provided by sponsors or other - preferably free or open source, and not private - libraries to help with your project. If you think you are in sketchy territory don't be shy to send us a email before the hackathon.

Does CrimsonCode Provide Travel Reimburstment?

Our budget simply doesnt allow for us to provide travel reimbursement at this time. Sorry for any inconvenience
